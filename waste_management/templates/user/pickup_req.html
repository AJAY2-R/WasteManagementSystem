Instructions for Packing
Step-by-Step Instructions:
  1. Separate the non-biodegradable waste into different categories based on the type of materials, such as plastic, metal, glass, paper, and other materials.
  2. Get appropriate containers or bags for each category of waste. Ensure they are sturdy and suitable for containing the specific waste material.
  3. Create or obtain labels for each container or bag, clearly indicating the type of non-biodegradable waste it will hold (e.g., "Plastic," "Metal," "Glass," "Paper").
  4. If there are any sharp or hazardous non-biodegradable items (e.g., broken glass, sharp metal), wrap them securely in old newspaper or bubble wrap before placing them in their respective containers.
  5. Begin filling each container or bag with the corresponding type of non-biodegradable waste. Make sure to avoid mixing different materials to facilitate recycling later.
  6. Once each container or bag is filled, securely seal them to prevent any spills or leaks during transportation.
  7. If you plan to store the waste before disposal or recycling, keep the containers in a designated area that is safe and separate from other materials.
  8. If transporting the waste, load the sealed containers carefully into a vehicle, making sure they are stable to avoid any accidents during transit.
  9. Dispose of the non-biodegradable waste in accordance with local regulations and guidelines. If possible, prioritize recycling by taking the waste to a recycling center or using curbside recycling services.
  10. If any non-biodegradable items can be reused or repurposed, consider doing so to minimize waste.
  11. After packing and disposing of the non-biodegradable waste, ensure the packing area is clean and tidy.
  12. Review the process to identify any improvements for future waste management.
